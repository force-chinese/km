AI Core Research & KM Methodology

Dual-Track System
Architecture & Philosophy

Moving from "Information Retrieval" to "Closed-Loop Knowledge Management".
A standardized framework for policy researchers and consultants.

1. Overview: The Dual-Track System

Upstream and Downstream Collaboration, Not Competition.

The most common pitfall in the AI era is using a single tool for both "Finding Data" and "Synthesizing Research." This leads to hallucinations and fragmented knowledge.

  • Type 1 (Search AI): The Scout/Hunter. Responsible for divergent discovery of external evidence.
  • Type 2 (NotebookLM): The Lab. Responsible for convergent reasoning within a controlled environment.

2. Type 1: Search AI (Scout / Hunter)

External Exploration â€¢ Rapid Verification â€¢ No Final Conclusions

Role Allocation of 4 Key Tools

1. Google Search AI

Baseline: Free, real-time, official-source prioritized. Best for initial keyword scoping.

2. Google Deep Research

Scanner: Deep scanning for policy landscapes and multi-year reports. Finds the "long-tail" documents.

3. Perplexity

Precision: Pinpoint verification of specific numbers (e.g., 2024 Subsidy Amount) and source validation.

4. Felo (or Language Specialist AI)

Translator: Specialized in converting bureaucratic policy language into structured business terms.

âš ï¸ Risk Warning: Information here is fragmented and lacks an audit trail. Do not use for final decision-making.

3. Type 2: NotebookLM Closed-Loop KM

NotebookLM is the "Research Primary System,"
not a search engine, and not a chatbot.

3-1. Core Concept: Closed-Loop KM

Research is not a one-off Q&A session. It is a replayable, convergent, and upgradable cyclical system.

  • ğŸ”„ Replayable: Preserves the reasoning and dialogue trail of every iteration.
  • ğŸ“‰ Convergent: Noise is eliminated, and sources become cleaner with every loop.
  • ğŸš€ Upgradable: Conclusions from one cycle become inputs for the next.

"Type 2 is a self-evolving Knowledge Management system."

1. Built-in Deep Research

Uses system-recommended sources. Advantages: Traceable research path and direct links to original documents.

2. Manual Import
